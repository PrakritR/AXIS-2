- **Minimum cleaning charge:** $100 — light cleaning, surface wipe-down, minor trash removal
- **Standard cleaning charge:** $150 — full room clean, vacuuming/mopping, bathroom scrub, appliance wipe-down
- **Heavy cleaning charge:** $250 — deep clean required due to excessive mess, strong odors, unsanitary conditions, or large quantities of debris

**8.1 Kitchen and Food Storage.**

(a) **Assigned storage.** Each resident is assigned dedicated refrigerator and pantry/cabinet storage corresponding to their room number. (b) **Food labeling.** All food items stored in shared refrigerator or pantry areas must be clearly labeled with the Resident's name and the date stored. Unlabeled food in shared areas may be discarded after 48 hours without liability to Landlord.

(c) **Dishes and surfaces.** Resident shall wash all dishes, pots, pans, and utensils immediately after use and return them to their designated location. (d) **Food in common areas.** No food shall be left unsecured, uncovered, or unattended in common areas. All perishable items must be stored within Resident's designated refrigerator compartment.

**8.2 Trash and Common Area Cleanliness.**

(a) **Shared trash responsibility.** All residents share responsibility for ensuring that common area trash receptacles (kitchen, living room, hallways) are emptied when full. No resident may allow a trash bin to overflow or leave trash bags on the floor. Resident shall take out trash whenever any bin is full, regardless of who filled it, and shall replace the trash liner promptly.

(b) **No personal items in common areas.** Personal belongings — including clothing, shoes, bags, equipment, boxes, or any other items — shall not be left in common areas (hallways, living room, kitchen, bathrooms, or laundry area) for more than **24 hours**. (c) **Cleaning charge for unresolved violations.** If Resident leaves the kitchen, bathroom, or any other shared area in an unclean or cluttered condition and fails to remedy the condition within **24 hours of receiving written notice** (which may be delivered by text message or email), Landlord may arrange for cleaning and charge Resident the actual cost of such cleaning per the schedule in Section 4.1(d). The 24-hour clock begins upon delivery of written notice, not upon discovery.

**8.3 Quiet Hours.**

(a) **Weeknights.** Quiet hours in all common areas and shared spaces are **10:00 PM – 8:00 AM**, Sunday through Thursday. During quiet hours, Resident shall keep noise levels in all common areas (kitchen, living room, hallway, laundry room, and bathrooms) at a level that does not disturb other residents.

(b) **Weekends.** Quiet hours on Friday and Saturday nights are **12:00 AM – 9:00 AM**. During all other weekend hours, Resident shall remain mindful of the shared living environment.

(c) **In-room noise.** Resident agrees to maintain reasonable noise levels within their private room at all times and to comply with quiet hours in all areas of the property.

**8.4 General House Rules.** Resident agrees to:

- Not host gatherings of more than **4 guests** in the property without prior written notice to Landlord
- Dispose of trash and recycling in designated containers on scheduled collection days
- Promptly report maintenance issues, leaks, or safety hazards to Landlord in writing
- Not block hallways, exits, or shared pathways with personal property at any time
- Respect all other residents' right to quiet enjoyment of the Premises

**8.5 Enforcement.** Failure to comply with these house rules after written notice may constitute a violation of this Agreement and grounds for termination pursuant to **RCW 59.18.180**. **Addendum A — Property Condition Baseline.**
This Addendum reinforces Section 5.1. The signed move-in checklist and any dated photographs or video recordings shall establish the baseline condition of the Premises for purposes of security deposit determinations. Resident shall not alter, damage, or obstruct smoke alarms, carbon monoxide alarms, or related safety systems when hanging décor or otherwise.

---

**Addendum B — Bed Bugs and Cooperation.**
Resident shall report any suspected bed-bug activity immediately in writing and shall fully cooperate with inspection and certified treatment protocols, including preparation, bagging of laundry, and providing reasonable access. If an infestation is reasonably attributable to Resident's conduct, guests, or failure to follow prevention or treatment instructions after written notice, Resident shall be responsible for reasonable and documented treatment costs, to the extent permitted by law.

---

**Addendum C — Mold, Moisture, and Ventilation.**
Resident shall use bathroom and kitchen ventilation when cooking or showering, remove standing condensation, keep personal property and textiles away from damp surfaces, and report any leaks or suspected mold within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery. Resident shall not paint over, conceal, or otherwise disturb mold. Landlord shall respond to building-system leaks in accordance with RCW 59.18.070, where applicable.

---

**Addendum D — Extended Resident Maintenance.**
Resident shall replace HVAC filters in accordance with Landlord's schedule, or at least every ninety (90) days for standard disposable one-inch (1") filters. Resident shall keep refrigerator coils reasonably clean and dust-free, operate garbage disposals only with running cold water, clear hair from accessible drain stoppers, promptly report running toilets, and comply with all posted recycling and composting rules.

---

**Addendum E — Rules and Nuisance Enforcement.**

(a) **Warning system.** Upon a first violation of house rules (including quiet hours, cleanliness standards, guest policies, or nuisance behavior), Landlord shall issue a written notice to cure. Resident shall have 24 hours to remedy the violation. Upon a second violation of the same or similar rule within any 60-day period, Landlord may assess charges as described in subsection (b) and/or issue a notice to comply or vacate pursuant to RCW 59.18.180.

(b) **Charges tied to actual costs.** Any charges assessed for rule violations shall be equal to the **reasonable and documented actual cost incurred by Landlord** as a direct result of the violation. This may include: cost of professional cleaning (per the schedule in Section 4.1(d)), cost of repairs, cost of third-party services, or costs incurred in remediating damage or disturbance. Landlord shall provide Resident with documentation of such costs (receipt, invoice, or written estimate from a third party) at the time the charge is assessed. Fixed penalties not tied to actual costs are not intended.

(c) **Escalation.** Repeated or uncured violations of quiet hours, harassment of other residents, or sustained unreasonable noise may result in termination proceedings pursuant to RCW 59.18.180 following proper notice.

(d) **Independent obligations.** A breach by other residents shall not excuse Resident's performance under this Agreement unless Landlord, after documented written notice, fails to take action within Landlord's legal authority to address such conduct.
